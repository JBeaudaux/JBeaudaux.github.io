How to Use a Defibrillator

Don't feel afraid about doing something wrong, the most important is to do something.
You are around when someone has sudden cardiac arrest, or you want to learn how to react in such a situation, you can follow a few simple steps to use a defibrillator and perform life-saving techniques.
You will be guided through 5 simple steps. At each step, clear instructions will be provided. A video also summarises the actions to perform.

Check vitals

Before using a defibrillator, check to ensure that the victim is in cardiac arrest.
  • Consciousness : Check to see if the victim is unable to respond. Try to wake him up.
  • Breathing : Lean in closely to listen for breathing. Look to see if his chest is rising and falling.
  • Pulse : Check the victim's pulse by placing the tips of your index and middle fingers on the person's wrist, at the base of the thumb.
If the victim is unresponsive and you find no pulse or breath, you may need to use the defibrillator.

Call emergency services

Explain to them where you are and what is going on. They will help and guide you to handle the situation at best, and send a team to take over. If there is someone else there other than you, make them call while you start working on the person in need.
Meanwhile, open the defibrillator's cover as shown on diagram. The device will start and guide you using vocal messages and visual indications.

Apply both electrodes

Remove clothes from the patient's upper body. Do not hesitate to cut them if necessary.
Apply the electrodes on patient's bare chest as shown on diagram:
  • One electrode is placed on the right, below the clavicle.
  • Another electrode is applied to the left side of the patient, just below and to the left of the pectoral muscle.

Wait for heart rhythm analysis

Do not touch the victim, and wait for the defibrillator to analyse heart rhythm. The analysis will starts without any intervention. Do not worry, the defibrillator will detect fibrilaltion and only shock if necessary.
Before a shock delivery, the defibrillator will warn you. In semi-automatic mode, the device informs you to deliver the shock by pressing the "shock" button. In automatic mode, it alerts you that the shock will be delivered without your intervention.

Cardiac massage

whether or not a shock was required, perform a cardiac massage on the victim. The device will inform you when and how to make a chest compression.
  • Place the heel of one hand on center of victim's chest.
  • Place the other hand on top.
  • Press firmly in time with the metronome beat, until next instruction.
A metronome will guide you to perform cardiac massage in the right rhythm.
